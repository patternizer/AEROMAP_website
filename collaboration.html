Collaborate with the AEROMAP team

AEROMAP's global aerosol model calculates aerosol microphysical parameters on the daily timescale and over the whole Earth surface at a spatial resolution of 1x1 degree. It's neural networks convert daily satellite measurements of optical depth and columnar water vapour to aerosol products that include the volume size distribution and its derived parameters (the volume concentration of fine and coarse particles as well as their geometric radii) in each pixel. From a multiparametric weighting of these parameters, AEROMAP also calculates 2 air quality indices (normalized to the international 1-10 scale): one as a measure of the hazard to human health and the second as a measure of the impact on visibility. AEROMAP's model has been validated for 10 distinct aerosol types/mixtures including: desert dust, biomass burning products, marine aerosol and urban pollution. We warmly welcome collaborations.

Requests for simulated data

The methodologies developed by AEROMAP are published in open access peer-reviewed journals. While the code used to generate AEROMAP's models is not open source, some useful MATLAB routines written by the fellow are available at the Researchers' Gateway. If you would like us to run our simulations for a particular time period or at a particular location feel free to contact us: Email AEROMAP .
